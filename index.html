The Problem

Dehydration is invisible ,
until it's too late.

Current tools either replace equipment athletes love, violate sponsorship deals, or infer hydration from sweat. None measure actual fluid intake. Coaches operate completely blind.

Smart Bottles Don't Work

Athletes have their bottle. Smart bottles violate Gatorade/Powerade sponsorships and create adoption friction no program wants.

Wearables Only Guess

WHOOP, Garmin, Oura, all infer hydration from sweat. No direct measurement. No real-time intervention capability.

Manual Tracking Fails

Compliance is near zero. Coaches see only lagging indicators, after the damage is done and the injury has happened.

53%

of NCAA Division I athletes begin practice already hypohydrated, before they've even broken a sweat.

10-20%

performance degradation from dehydration alone. More than most injuries.

Wardenaar et al., 2016 · Deloitte, 2024

How It Works

Attach. Drink. Know.

No behavior change. No bottle replacement. syp clips onto any water bottle and gives you, and your entire team, real-time, verified hydration data.

01
Hardware

Universal Attachment

syp's patent-pending load cell sleeve attaches to any water bottle in seconds. Works with existing equipment. No sponsorship conflicts. Zero athlete resistance.

02
Software

Real-Time Measurement

Direct fluid intake measurement syncs automatically via Bluetooth. Personalized targets based on body metrics, activity, and climate, not sweat-based guesses.

03
Intelligence

Team Visibility

Coaches see the full team on a live dashboard. At-risk alerts fire before performance degrades. Real data. Real decisions. Zero guesswork.

The Team

Built by athletes.
Engineered by scientists.

Mathis Fürst Haugen

CEO & Co-Founder
LinkedIn
Mathis is a Norwegian-born D1 soccer captain and Environmental Engineering student at the University of South Florida. His freshman year, a season-ending injury traced back to chronic dehydration showed him firsthand that the solutions athletes had access to were generic, non-personalized, and outdated. That experience became the foundation for syp.

As CEO, Mathis leads the team, hardware development, fundraising strategy, and commercial partnerships. He has piloted syp with 60+ D1 athletes, represented the company at the Florida Venture Forum and Web Summit in Lisbon, and won four university pitch competitions. A competitive soccer player, cross-country skier, and windsurfer, he didn't just build syp. He needed it.

Rima El Brouzi

CTO & Co-Founder
LinkedIn
Rima is a Moroccan-born computer scientist and graduate researcher at the University of South Florida, where she is completing her Master's in Computer Science with a concentration in AI and medical engineering. She brings rare depth to syp's technical foundation, having conducted AI research at USF's Machine Learning and Human Reasoning Lab, interned as a machine learning engineer at Fater AI in Paris, and served as a graduate assistant for USF Men's Soccer, where she saw the hydration problem up close alongside the athletes syp is built for.

As CTO, Rima leads all software development, AI model research, and the data infrastructure that turns raw intake measurements into actionable intelligence. She also works directly with syp's founding athletes, bridging the gap between what the technology does and what athletes actually need.

An international woman in STEM building at the intersection of sports, AI, and human performance, Rima is the technical engine behind everything syp does.
